<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="B">B</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: B">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><a href="/wiki/Benthic_zone" title="Benthic zone">Benthic</a>: bottom-dwelling, living on the sea bed</li>

<li>Bicuspid: having two cusps or points</li>

<li>Branchiostegal membrane: The membrane connecting the branchiostegal rays and enclosing the gill chamber ventrally.</li>

<li>Bristle: a stiff hair-like projection</li>
<li>Buckler: a bony shield</li>
<li>Bycatch: species other than the target species that are caught incidentally in a trawl</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="C">C</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: C">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Caniniform: shaped like a canine tooth, conical in form</li>
<li>Carapace: a horny or bony covering encasing the body</li>
<li>Cardiform: small set conical outgrowths in a close set patch or band; usually refers to a band of close set small conical teeth</li>
<li>Carinate: keeled, having a ridge along the middle line</li>
<li>Caruncle: a fleshy outgrowth</li>
<li>Caudal: pertaining to the tail</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fish_anatomy#Fins" title="Fish anatomy">Caudal fin</a>: the tail fin</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fish_anatomy" title="Fish anatomy">Caudal peduncle</a>: the region of the body between the end of the anal fin and the base of the caudal fin</li>

<li>Cirri: small, thin appendages, often subdivided into branches.</li>

<li>Coalesced: grown together</li>
<li>Compressed: flattened laterally</li>
<li>Confluent: joined together</li>
<li>Conical: cone shaped, with a cylindrical base and a pointed tip</li>
<li>Corselet: a scaly covering behind the pectorals of some fishes</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Countershading" title="Countershading">Countershading</a>: body colouration which is dark above and lighter below</li>
<li>Crenulate: having the edge slightly scalloped</li>
<li>Cutaneous: pertaining to the skin</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ctenoid_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Ctenoid scale">Ctenoid scale</a>: rough-edged scale</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cycloid_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Cycloid scale">Cycloid scale</a>: smooth-edged scale</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="D">D</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: D">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Deciduous: temporary, falling off</li>
<li>Demersal: living on or near the sea bed</li>
<li>Dendritic: resembling a tree or shrub</li>

<li>Dentate: with tooth-like projections</li>
<li>Denticle, denticulate: a little tooth, having an edge with small projecting teeth, the <a href="/wiki/Placoid_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Placoid scale">placoid scales</a> of cartilaginous fishes</li>

<li>Dermal: pertaining to the skin</li>

<li>Distal: remote from the point of attachment</li>

<li>Dorsum: the upper (dorsal) surface of the head or body</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fish_anatomy#Fins" title="Fish anatomy">Dorsal fin</a>: the fin on a fish's back</li>
<li>Dorsal fin origin: the most anterior point of the dorsal fin base</li></ul>

<li>Erectile: capable of being raised or erected, often referring to spines</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anglerfish" title="Anglerfish">Esca</a>: the lure or "bait" on the end of the illicium of some anglerfishes and relatives</li>
<li>Estuarine: living in estuaries</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F">F</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: F">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="G">G</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: G">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ganoid_scale" class="mw-redirect" title="Ganoid scale">Ganoid scales</a>: Armor-like scales coated with ganoin found in gars and bichirs.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Genital_papilla" title="Genital papilla">Genital papilla</a>: a small, fleshy tube behind the anus in some fishes, from which the sperm or eggs are released; the sex of a fish often can be determined by the shape of its papilla.</li>
<li>Gill arches: the bony arches to which the gills are attached</li>
<li>Gill cover: or bony flap that covers the gills, the operculum</li>
<li>Gill filaments: a series of projections along the posterior edge of the gill arch, the site of gas exchange</li>
<li>Gill membranes: membranes covering the gill openings, attached to the branchiostegals</li>
<li>Gill opening: opening behind each operculum, leading to the gills</li>
<li>Gill rakers: a series of appendages along the anterior edges of the gill arches</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gill" title="Gill">Gills</a>, Branchiae: organs for breathing the oxygen contained in water, and for excreting carbon dioxide from the blood</li>
<li>Glossohyal: the tongue bone</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gonopodium" class="mw-redirect" title="Gonopodium">Gonopodium</a>: a modification of the anal fin of males of certain live-bearing species in the families <a href="/wiki/Anablepidae" title="Anablepidae">Anablepidae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poeciliidae" title="Poeciliidae">Poeciliidae</a>. It is used to transfer reproductive products to the female during mating.</li>
<li>Gravid: Sexually ripe</li>
<li>Gular region: pertaining to the region behind the chin and between the sides of the lower jaw</li></ul>

<li>Hyaline: translucent or transparent</li>
<li>Hypural joint: the joint between the caudal fin and the last of the vertebrae</li>
<li>Hypural plate: the flattened bony plate at the posterior end of the vertebral column, formed from parts of the posterior vertebrae.</li></ul>

<li>Supraorbital tentacle: a flap or filament of skin positioned above the eye</li>
<li>Suprascapular: a bone uniting the shoulder girdle with the skull</li>
<li>Suture: the line of union of two bones or plates</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Swimbladder" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimbladder">Swimbladder</a>: a sac filled with gas, lying beneath the backbone</li>
<li>Symphysis: point of junction of the two sides of the jaw</li>
<li>Symmetrical: similarly arranged on both sides</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="T">T</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_ichthyology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: T">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Teleost" title="Teleost">Teleost</a>: a member of Teleostei, an infraclass containing most of the <a href="/wiki/Actinopterygii" title="Actinopterygii">bony fishes</a></li>
<li>Terminal: at the end</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tessellation" title="Tessellation">Tessellated</a>: marked with little checks or squares, like tiles</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thorax" title="Thorax">Thoracic</a>: pertaining to the chest</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thorax" title="Thorax">Thorax</a>: the chest region, just behind the head</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fish_measurement" title="Fish measurement">Total length</a>: the length from the tip of the snout to the tip of the tail</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Transverse_plane" title="Transverse plane">Transverse</a>: crosswise</li>
<li>Trilobate: with three lobes</li>
<li>Tricuspid: with three cusps or points</li>
<li>Truncate: terminating abruptly, as if cut off square</li>
<li>Trunk: the region of a fish between the head and tail, or the last gill slit and vent</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tubercle" title="Tubercle">Tubercle</a>: a small, usually hard excrescence or lump</li>
<li>Tubiform, tubuliform: resembling a tube</li>
<li>Type locality: the location from which the <a href="/wiki/Type_(zoology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Type (zoology)">type</a> specimen was collected.</li></ul>
